# /marketing-psychology — 知 (Knowing)

Part of the **知 (Knowing)** loop: understand *why people buy* so you can make true value
legible — never to deceive. Pure advisory: no render, no paid API. Helps you decide which
psychological lever fits a real situation, then apply it honestly.

## Hard rules
- **Pure local advisory.** No engine, no render, no paid API.
- **Ethical use only.** Several of these models can manipulate. Use them to make *true* value
  legible, never to mislead. **Scarcity, social proof, authority, and urgency claims must be
  REAL** — no invented stats, fake reviews, fabricated waitlists, or phantom "low stock". See
  `framework/operating-principles.md` (no fabrication, anti-hype).
- **Voice + audience.** Keep your own voice and your real buyer in mind when this informs content.

## Advisory process (how to run)
1. **Diagnose** — which models apply to the user's situation (use the quick-reference table).
2. **Explain** — the psychology behind each chosen model in one or two lines.
3. **Apply** — the specific marketing move for this product/audience.
4. **Implement ethically** — how to do it honestly, and the failure mode to avoid.
Close with the task-specific questions if the situation is underspecified.

## Foundational thinking models
First Principles (5 Whys → root cause) · Jobs to Be Done (buy the hole, not the drill) · Circle of Competence · Inversion (what guarantees failure? avoid it) · Occam's Razor (check the obvious first) · Pareto 80/20 · Local vs Global Optima (zoom out before in) · Theory of Constraints (fix the one bottleneck) · Opportunity Cost · Diminishing Returns · Second-Order Thinking (a flash sale trains discount-waiting) · Map ≠ Territory · Probabilistic Thinking · Barbell Strategy (80% proven + 20% bets, avoid the mediocre middle).

## Buyer psychology
Fundamental Attribution Error (examine your process before blaming buyers) · Mere Exposure · Availability Heuristic (case studies make success imaginable) · Confirmation Bias · Lindy Effect (fundamentals outlast fads) · Mimetic Desire · Sunk Cost (kill underperformers) · Endowment Effect (free trial → ownership) · IKEA Effect (let them build it) · Zero-Price Effect ($0 ≫ $1) · Hyperbolic Discounting (emphasize immediate benefit) · Status-Quo Bias (reduce switch friction) · Default Effect · Paradox of Choice (3 tiers > 7) · Goal-Gradient (progress bars) · Peak-End Rule · Zeigarnik (open loops; "80% done") · Pratfall (a small admitted flaw → trust) · Curse of Knowledge (test copy with newcomers) · Mental Accounting ("$3/day" vs "$90/mo") · Regret Aversion (guarantees) · Bandwagon / Social Proof.

## Persuasion / influence
Reciprocity (give first) · Commitment & Consistency (small commitment first) · Authority Bias · Liking / Similarity · Unity ("one of us") · Scarcity / Urgency (genuine only) · Foot-in-the-Door (trial → paid → annual) · Door-in-the-Face (big ask, then retreat) · Loss Aversion / Prospect Theory (losses ~2× gains) · Anchoring (show the high price first) · Decoy Effect · Framing ("90% success" vs "10% failure") · Contrast Effect (show the before).

## Pricing psychology
Charm Pricing / Left-Digit ($99 reads ≪ $100 — value products) · Rounded-Price Fluency (round = premium, $500/mo) · Rule of 100 (<$100 → "20% off"; >$100 → "$50 off") · Price Relativity / Good-Better-Best (middle = target) · Mental Accounting ("less than your morning coffee"). *(Psychology frames the number; it doesn't set it — keep your own pricing model.)*

## Design & delivery
Hick's Law (one CTA > three) · AIDA (Attention→Interest→Desire→Action) · Rule of 7 (~7 touchpoints) · Nudge / Choice Architecture · BJ Fogg (Behavior = Motivation × Ability × Prompt) · EAST (Easy, Attractive, Social, Timely) · COM-B (Capability, Opportunity, Motivation) · Activation Energy (pre-fill, templates, quick wins) · North Star Metric · Cobra Effect (incentives can backfire — test them).

## Growth & scaling
Feedback Loops · Compounding (start early) · Network Effects · Flywheel (content→traffic→leads→customers→case studies→content) · Switching Costs (ethical lock-in) · Exploration vs Exploitation · Critical Mass / Tipping Point (depth before breadth) · Survivorship Bias (study the failures too).

## Task-specific questions (ask when the situation is underspecified)
What specific behavior are you trying to influence? What does the customer believe before they meet your marketing? Where in the journey (awareness → consideration → decision) is this? What's currently preventing the desired action? Have you tested this with real customers?
