Speaker (fictional): Dana Lim, a composite "operator-educator" used only to demonstrate the format.

Rough notes from the talk:

- "Nobody wants your tool. They want the thing on the other side of it. Sell the other side."
- People don't buy a CRM, they buy 'I stopped losing leads at 2am'. Name the 2am moment.
- Price the outcome, not the hours. If the outcome is worth RM10k to them, RM1k is a bargain and
  RM100 is suspicious.
- Her test before launching anything: "Can I describe the after-state in one sentence a tired person
  would nod at?"
- On first offers: charge for the transformation, deliver it small, prove it once, then raise.
- Warning she repeated twice: don't invent the after-state. If you haven't delivered it for one real
  person yet, say "first cohort" and price honestly.
